Classic Pecan Pie Recipe

Servings and Timing

Servings: 8

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 50–60 minutes

Total Time: About 1 hour 20 minutes, plus cooling

Ingredients

For the Crust (or use a store-bought 9-inch pie crust):

  • 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 3–4 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup light corn syrup (or dark for a deeper flavor)
  • ⅓ cup unsalted butter, melted and slightly cooled
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 ½ cups pecan halves (or coarsely chopped for easier slicing)
  • Optional: 1–2 tablespoons bourbon or dark rum for extra depth (a common Southern touch)

Equipment

  • 9-inch pie dish
  • Mixing bowls
  • Whisk or electric mixer
  • Rolling pin (if making homemade crust)
  • Pie weights or dried beans (for blind baking)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Crust:

    • In a large bowl, whisk flour, sugar, and salt. Cut in cold butter using a pastry cutter or fork until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized butter pieces.
    • Add ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough just comes together. Form into a disc, wrap in plastic, and chill for at least 1 hour (or up to 2 days).
    • Roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface to a 12-inch circle. Transfer to a 9-inch pie dish, trim excess, and crimp edges. Chill for 30 minutes to prevent shrinking.
    • Preheat oven to 375°F. Line the crust with parchment paper, fill with pie weights or dried beans, and blind bake for 15 minutes. Remove weights and parchment, then bake for 5 more minutes. Set aside to cool slightly.

  2. Make the Filling:

    • In a large bowl, whisk eggs and sugar until smooth. Add corn syrup, melted butter, vanilla, salt, and bourbon (if using). Stir until fully combined.
    • Stir in pecans, ensuring they’re evenly distributed.

  3. Assemble and Bake:

    • Pour the filling into the partially baked crust. Arrange any pecans on top for a prettier presentation if desired.
    • Bake at 350°F for 50–60 minutes, until the center is set but slightly jiggly (it’ll firm up as it cools). If the crust browns too quickly, cover edges with foil or a pie shield after 30 minutes.
    • Cool completely on a wire rack (at least 4 hours) before slicing to ensure clean cuts.

  4. Serve:

    • Slice and serve at room temperature or slightly warmed, with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream for that classic holiday feel.

    Tips for Grandma’s Touch

    • Corn Syrup Choice: Light corn syrup gives a milder, sweeter flavor; dark corn syrup adds a molasses-like depth. If you’re unsure what your grandmother used, light is a safe bet for a classic taste.
    • Toasted Pecans: For extra flavor, lightly toast pecans in a dry skillet over medium heat for 3–5 minutes before adding to the filling.
    • Crust Variations: If your grandmother’s pie had a particularly flaky crust, ensure your butter stays cold and avoid overworking the dough. For a shortcut, a high-quality store-bought crust works well.
    • Storage: Store the pie at room temperature for up to 2 days or in the fridge for up to 4 days, covered tightly. Reheat slices gently in a 300°F oven for 10 minutes to revive the texture.
